Symbolic
Risk: None
[1] [2] [3] [4] [5]
Predicates, ontologies, PDDL, behaviour trees — deterministic, auditable. Zero hallucination tolerance. Best for safety-critical deployments with well-enumerated failure modes.
Sub-Symbolic
Risk: High
[6] [7] [8] [9] [10] [11] [12] [13] [14]
Neural embeddings, LLMs, Seq2Seq — learned from data, generalisable. High hallucination risk. Best for open-ended, novel failure environments where linguistic naturalness is paramount.
Hybrid
Risk: Low
[5]✦ [15] [16] [17] [18] [19] [20] [21]
Symbolic constraints guide neural generation — combines reliability and fluency. Contextually optimal, not unconditionally superior. ✦ [5] cross-listed from Symbolic. Best when both correctness & fluency required.
